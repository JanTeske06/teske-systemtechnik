002 / How it runs

How the call runs

  1. 01

    Inquiry

    You send a short inquiry with the question or problem. Reply within 24 h, with a proposed time and a hint about which tier (30 or 60 min) makes sense.

  2. 02

    Optional prep

    Send material ahead, code repo, screenshots, an existing tool, and I look at it before the call. Makes the minutes count more, no surcharge.

  3. 03

    The call

    30 or 60 minutes, depending on the chosen tier. Live (video or audio) or async (audio messages plus notes, if time zones are tricky).

  4. 04

    Summary

    Within 24 h after the call: written summary, architecture recommendation, cost estimate where applicable. By email.

005 / Frequent questions

What you should know about the call

Why not a free call? +

Because a good call needs preparation, reading code, weighing options, sketching architecture. Plus, the price filters out inquiries where nobody really wants to invest. Result: a call that delivers something, not a 15-minute sales pitch.

And if you say it's not feasible? +

Then you have the written summary with reasoning and save yourself a project that wouldn't have worked. That's the most common "worst case", and the call still paid off.

When are 30 minutes enough, when do I need 60? +

30 work if you already have a concrete question ("Should I use X or Y?"). 60 is useful for more complex workflows or if we want to look at live code together.

How does the project credit work? +

The call price shows up in the project invoice as "already paid". You pay the project fixed price minus €75 (or €150). Credit applies to any follow-up project with me.

Does async work? +

Yes. Audio messages and shared notes, if live calls are tricky. Same price, same deliverable.

What if the call overruns? +

5–10 minutes of buffer are planned. If it needs more: either schedule a follow-up, or upgrade to the 60-minute tier on the spot, you pay the difference, nothing twice.
